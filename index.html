Hi, I’m Sandra Becker, and I love digging into data. Datawrapper’s recent piece on the world’s first trillionaire sparked the idea for this post.

I wanted to take an additional path let's say a side chain: How exceptional is Elon Musk’s rise? Is it genuinely anomalous, or does he simply occupy the extreme end of a pattern shared by the world’s wealthiest people?

Musk’s enormous fortune is nothing new. What is remarkable is the scale and shape of this latest jump. Through the SpaceX IPO, his estimated net worth rose from roughly $342 billion—already an almost incomprehensible sum—to $1.1 trillion.

That is an increase of about 222%: more than a tripling of his wealth in a single event. Fortunes usually grow incrementally, even at the very top. This was something different—less a continuation of an existing trajectory than a sudden, exponential leap. So the question is not simply how rich Musk has become. It is whether the mechanism and magnitude of his rise place him outside the historical pattern altogether.
